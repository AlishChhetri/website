Secretary

ACM Allegheny College Student Chapter | September 2024 - May 2025

Role Overview

As Secretary of the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) student chapter at Allegheny College, I help lead the organization, document its activities, and coordinate communication between members, faculty advisors, and the broader community. The ACM chapter provides a platform for students interested in computer science to connect, learn, and grow through hands-on activities, industry connections, and collaborative projects.

Key Accomplishments

  • Co-founded the ACM chapter to foster student collaboration in software development and hands-on learning
  • Organized Allegheny College's first-ever hackathon, bringing together students across disciplines
  • Hosted workshops that bridge academic concepts with real-world software development practices
  • Maintained detailed records of chapter meetings, events, and membership information
  • Facilitated communication between chapter leadership, members, and faculty advisors

Skills and Competencies Developed

  • Leadership: Helping guide the chapter's direction and inspiring member participation
  • Event Organization: Planning and executing technical workshops, hackathons, and guest speaker sessions
  • Documentation: Creating and maintaining clear records of chapter activities
  • Community Building: Fostering a collaborative environment for students with shared interests
  • Communication: Serving as a liaison between different stakeholders in the campus community

Impact and Initiatives

My work with the ACM chapter has created positive change in our campus community:

  • Established a sustainable organization that provides opportunities for students interested in computing
  • Created bridges between classroom learning and industry practices through professional development events
  • Fostered networking opportunities between students and computing professionals
  • Provided a platform for students to develop and showcase technical projects
  • Promoted diversity and inclusion in computing through targeted outreach and accessible events
